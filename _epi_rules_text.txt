 CROP DISEASE
 
EPIDEMIOLOGICAL RULES
 Temperature · Humidity · Rainfall · Wind · Season · Management
 
 60 Crop-Disease Profiles across 13 Crops
 
Purpose: This reference document compiles epidemiological decision rules for 60 crop-disease combinations across 13
economically important crops. Each profile specifies the critical temperature range, relative humidity threshold,
rainfall/leaf-wetness requirement, dispersal mechanism, high-risk season, pathogen identity, infection trigger conditions,
and integrated management options. These rules can be directly embedded into real-time disease-risk advisory systems,
early-warning dashboards, or AI-based crop protection models.
Field Definitions
FIELD
MEANING
Temperature
Air temperature range favouring infection/sporulation (°C)
Relative Humidity
% RH threshold at/above which infection probability rises sharply
Rainfall/Wetness
Rain amount or leaf-wetness duration that triggers infection cycles
Wind/Dispersal
Primary spore/pathogen dispersal mechanism
High-Risk Season
Crop growth stage or calendar period of peak risk
High-Risk Trigger
Quantitative rule or threshold that defines epidemic onset conditions
Management
Key chemical, cultural, and varietal control options

BEAN
■   Angular Leaf Spot
PATHOGEN
Phaeoisariopsis griseola (Fungi – Deuteromycetes)
TEMPERATURE
16 – 28 °C (opt. 22 °C)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
>85 % RH
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Moderate rain; prolonged leaf wetness (>8 h)
WIND / DISPERSAL
Wind-driven rain spreads conidia
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Warm-wet growing season
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Risk peaks when night temps stay above 16 °C with morning dew lasting >6 h.
MANAGEMENT
Resistant varieties; copper-based fungicides; avoid overhead irrigation; crop rotation ≥2 yrs.
■   Rust
PATHOGEN
Uromyces appendiculatus (Basidiomycete)
TEMPERATURE
15 – 25 °C (opt. 20 °C)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
80 – 95 % RH
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Light rain / heavy dew; wet periods >6 h
WIND / DISPERSAL
Wind disperses urediniospores over long distances
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Cool-humid periods
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Urediniospore germination is optimal at 18–22 °C; spores killed above 32 °C.
MANAGEMENT
Mancozeb / triazole fungicides; early sowing to escape peak inoculum; destroy crop residues.
✔   Healthy
PATHOGEN / AGENT
N/A – No pathogen
OPTIMAL CONDITIONS
See notes
MANAGEMENT
Regular scouting; prophylactic measures to remain healthy.
NOTES
No disease pressure. Maintain balanced nutrition and irrigation for optimal yield.
COTTON

■   Bacterial Blight
PATHOGEN
Xanthomonas citri pv. malvacearum (Bacterium)
TEMPERATURE
28 – 34 °C (opt. 30 °C)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
>80 % RH
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Heavy rain + high humidity; water-soaked soils
WIND / DISPERSAL
Wind / rain splash disperses Xanthomonas
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Hot-humid monsoon period
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Storm events causing leaf wounding dramatically raise infection probability.
MANAGEMENT
Disease-free certified seed; copper oxychloride sprays; resistant cultivars (immune lines B-2, Stoneville
213).
■   Fusarium Wilt
PATHOGEN
Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. vasinfectum (Ascomycete)
TEMPERATURE
20 – 32 °C (opt. 28 °C)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
Soil moisture: 60–80 % FC
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Waterlogging / soil compaction predisposes plants
WIND / DISPERSAL
Soil movement; root-to-root contact
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Early season planting in warm soils
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Chlamydospores persist in soil >10 years; sandy soils with low pH (<6) increase severity.
MANAGEMENT
Wilt-resistant varieties; soil solarisation; seed treatment with Trichoderma; balanced K nutrition.
■   Leaf Curl Virus
PATHOGEN
Cotton Leaf Curl Virus – CLCuV (Begomovirus, ssDNA)
TEMPERATURE
28 – 35 °C
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
40 – 70 % RH (dry, warm favours vector)
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Low rainfall favours whitefly build-up
WIND / DISPERSAL
Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci) is primary vector
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Dry, hot conditions
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Vector population explodes when temp >30 °C and rainfall <25 mm/month.
MANAGEMENT
Vector management (imidacloprid seed treatment, neem sprays); resistant hybrids (NHH-44, MRC-7351);
remove infected plants.
✔   Healthy
PATHOGEN / AGENT
N/A – No pathogen
OPTIMAL CONDITIONS
See notes
MANAGEMENT
Regular scouting for early detection; balanced NPK; optimum plant spacing.
NOTES
Optimal growth: 25–35 °C, well-drained loam, pH 6–8. No disease pressure.

GRAPE
■   Bacterial Rot (Crown Gall)
PATHOGEN
Agrobacterium vitis (Rhizobiaceae)
TEMPERATURE
20 – 28 °C
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
High soil moisture
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Waterlogged soils; freeze-thaw wounding
WIND / DISPERSAL
Contaminated pruning tools; soil water
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Early spring / after frost injury
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Risk highest after winter freeze damage that creates wound entry points.
MANAGEMENT
Certified pathogen-free planting material; heat therapy of cuttings (50 °C, 30 min); biological control with
K1026 strain.
■   Black Rot
PATHOGEN
Guignardia bidwellii (Ascomycete)
TEMPERATURE
15 – 32 °C (opt. 26 °C)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
>85 % RH
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Frequent rain ≥2.5 mm; wet periods >6 h post-budbreak
WIND / DISPERSAL
Rain-splash disperses pycnidiospores
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Spring–early summer (budbreak to fruit set)
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Infection risk table (Mills chart): at 26 °C, only 6 h wet period needed; at 15 °C, 24 h needed.
MANAGEMENT
Mancozeb or myclobutanil from early shoot growth; canopy management for air circulation; remove
mummies.
■   Downy Mildew
PATHOGEN
Plasmopara viticola (Oomycete)
TEMPERATURE
12 – 25 °C (opt. 20 °C)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
>90 % RH
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Rain >10 mm triggers primary infection; overnight wetness
WIND / DISPERSAL
Wind disperses sporangia (Plasmopara viticola)
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Spring–autumn wet periods
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Primary (oospore) infection: ≥10 mm rain + soil temp >11 °C + avg temp >10 °C ('10-10-10 rule').
MANAGEMENT
Copper-based fungicides; phosphonate sprays; resistant rootstocks/varieties; leaf removal to improve
airflow.

■   Esca / Black Measles
PATHOGEN
Phaeomoniella chlamydospora + Phaeoacremonium spp. + Fomitiporia mediterranea (Basidiomycete)
complex
TEMPERATURE
25 – 35 °C (stress aggravates)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
Fluctuating moisture stress
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Drought then re-watering triggers apoplexy
WIND / DISPERSAL
Pruning wounds are entry points
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Mid-summer heat stress
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Apoplectic (rapid collapse) form triggered by water stress at >35 °C.
MANAGEMENT
Protect pruning wounds (Trichoderma paste); delayed pruning; remove and burn symptomatic wood; no
cure once established.
■   Leaf Blight (Isariopsis Leaf Spot)
PATHOGEN
Pseudocercospora vitis (syn. Isariopsis clavispora) (Fungi)
TEMPERATURE
23 – 30 °C
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
>80 % RH
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Warm-wet conditions post-veraison
WIND / DISPERSAL
Rain-splash of conidia
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Late season (post-veraison)
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Disease intensifies when post-harvest canopy remains wet with warm temperatures.
MANAGEMENT
Mancozeb or copper sprays; canopy management; vineyard sanitation at leaf fall.
■   Powdery Mildew
PATHOGEN
Erysiphe necator (Uncinula necator) (Ascomycete)
TEMPERATURE
20 – 27 °C (opt. 25 °C)
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
40 – 70 % RH (does NOT require rain; dry-warm favoured)
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Rain-free periods; moderate humidity
WIND / DISPERSAL
Wind disperses conidia – key spread mechanism
HIGH-RISK SEASON
Warm, dry summers with mild humid nights
HIGH-RISK TRIGGER
Unique: rain inhibits spore germination. Critical period: 20–25 °C + 40–70 % RH without rain.
MANAGEMENT
Sulfur (120–240 g/hl); DMI / SDHI fungicides; resistant varieties (e.g., Norton, Regent); early season
applications at budbreak.
✔   Healthy
PATHOGEN / AGENT
N/A – No pathogen
OPTIMAL CONDITIONS
See notes
MANAGEMENT
Annual soil tests; proper canopy management; preventative spray calendar.
NOTES
No disease pressure. Balanced irrigation and pruning maintain vine health.

■   Rosette
PATHOGEN
Groundnut Rosette Virus (GRV) + Groundnut Rosette Assistor Virus (GRAV) complex
TEMPERATURE
25 – 35 °C
RELATIVE HUMIDITY
Dry conditions favour aphid vector
RAINFALL / WETNESS
Low rainfall increases aphid populations
WIND / DISPERSAL

Early planting (within 2 weeks of rains); imidacloprid seed treatment; reflective mulch; resistant varieties
(ICGV-IS 96894).

Resistant varieties (Co 0238, CoS 8436); hot water treatment of setts; improve field drainage;

Airborne teliospores at anthesis
